What We Do

Research Themes

Our research focuses on developing technologies for monitoring, diagnostics, therapies and interventions for a variety of diseases and disorders, spanning novel microelectronics through to biomedical computational modelling.

Two key research areas underpin our work: novel microelectronics and microsystems, integrated with cutting-edge microfabrication, MEMS, optoelectronic and microfluidic technologies for miniature wearable and implantable devices; and biomedical computational modelling, bio-signal processing and imaging.

⚡
Sensing & Diagnostics
Wearable and implantable sensor platforms
▼

We develop miniature sensor platforms and diagnostic systems that bridge the gap between clinical-grade measurement and practical, deployable devices, from wearables to implantables.

Our sensing and diagnostics research covers:

  • Vital signs monitoring: continuous, wearable physiological sensing
  • Fast bio-signal decoding and processing: real-time algorithms for high-bandwidth biosignals
  • Lab-on-a-chip for point-of-care infectious diseases detection
  • Electrical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) for biomarker analysis
  • Electrical impedance tomography (EIT) for functional monitoring
  • Device safety monitoring via humidity and temperature sensing

Neural Interfaces
Closed-loop implantable systems for the nervous system
▼

Our neural interface research targets a broad range of neurological and neuromuscular conditions, from movement disorders to sensory deficits. We develop fully implantable systems that can both record and stimulate neural activity in a closed-loop manner.

Specific research activities include:

  • Fully implantable closed-loop neural interfaces for the central nervous system: Parkinson's disease, epilepsy, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis
  • Peripheral nervous system interfaces: incontinence, paralysis, hearing loss, balance disorder
  • Neural spike sorting and local field potential (LFP) analysis
  • Neural system modelling and computational neuroscience
  • Nerve repair by integrating microelectronics with stem cell technology

Drug Delivery
Intelligent miniaturised delivery systems
▼

We design and fabricate miniaturised drug delivery systems that leverage advanced microfabrication, MEMS and microfluidic technologies to enable precise, controlled and targeted therapeutic delivery.

  • Implantable micro-pump and micro-reservoir systems
  • Electrochemically-controlled release mechanisms
  • Integration with sensing feedback for closed-loop therapy
  • MEMS-based actuation for precise dose control

Energy Harvesting, Wireless Powering & Communication
Autonomous power and data strategies for implantables
▼

A fundamental challenge for implantable and wearable biomedical devices is sustainable, reliable power and data communication without large batteries or wired connections. We develop solutions across the full energy chain.

  • Inductive and capacitive wireless power transfer for deep-implanted devices
  • Piezoelectric, thermoelectric and RF energy harvesting
  • Ultra-low-power circuit design for always-on sensing
  • Near-field and mid-field wireless data communication
  • Co-design of power management and communication for implantable ASICs

Capabilities

Technologies & Methods

We combine expertise across multiple technology domains to deliver end-to-end research.

🔬

Microfabrication & MEMS

Clean-room fabrication of microelectrodes, microfluidic channels and MEMS structures for biological interfaces.

💻

ASIC & Circuit Design

Custom analogue and mixed-signal IC design for ultra-low-power biomedical front-ends and signal processors.

📐

Computational Modelling

Finite element models of electric fields in tissue, neural population models and signal processing algorithm development.

🌐

Optoelectronics & Microfluidics

Integration of photonic and fluidic components for optical biosensing, fluorescence detection and lab-on-a-chip systems.
